Anduin Lending

Revolut: moving — slowly.

Where we stand

Lending is committed — but not a 2026 focus topic for them.

Revolut leadership is heads-down on IPO preparation. New product initiatives outside the IPO narrative have been deprioritized. Our lending workstream has slipped, and the previously discussed go-live date has been pushed back.

Read

This is not a fade — it's a queue.

Nothing in the conversation indicates lost interest. The relationship is intact, the technical case still resonates, and they remain the named anchor for Lending. The constraint is bandwidth, not appetite.

Timeline
  • ●  Initial commitment — secured
  • ●  IPO prep absorbs bandwidth
  • ●  Go-live date pushed back
  • ○  Post-IPO re-engagement

Approach: keep the relationship warm, advance product, do not push for resource we know they don't have right now.

Pipeline Momentum

Slower than hoped — but never going backwards.

What's true

Every cycle is taking longer than our base case.

  • Regulated counterparties = quarter-long internal cycles, not week-long ones.
  • Decisions need legal, risk, and exec sign-off in parallel.
  • Q1 commitments still in technical-evaluation stage, not yet integrated.
What's also true

Movement is one-directional — toward us.

  • Lead list expanded 9 → 21 since the start of the year.
  • 3 committed clients (OSL, Bitvavo, Revolut) all still in.
  • New Tier 1 entries on the back of inbound interest, not just outbound.

The honest framing: if you scored us against the timeline we showed twelve months ago, we are behind. If you score us against where regulated-finance B2B sales actually clears — three committed anchors, a widening Tier 1, and zero churn — we are early but on the curve.

Build Velocity

AI changed the economics
of being ready early.

Two to three years ago, the build cost behind a settlement + lending + issuance stack was a team of 15 and 18 months of runway. That math is no longer true for us.

  • AI-assisted development compresses contract, adapter, and integration work by an order of magnitude.
  • We ship faster than the pipeline currently pulls — engineering is no longer the bottleneck.
  • When a Tier 1 client says "yes, build this for us," our time-to-integration is measured in weeks, not quarters.
