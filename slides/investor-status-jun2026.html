Product excursion · 1 of 3 · Leveraged exposure

Spot & high-leverage replacements, as securities.

ESMA product intervention on leverage applies to CFDs — not to these instruments structured as securities. That's the regulatory door we walk through; the product variety is what we put behind it.

KO Certificate

Knock-out Certificate

Long/short with an automatic knock-out barrier — liquidates cleanly when the underlying breaches a maintenance-margin equivalent. No margin call, no negative balance.

Sells because: the perp's leverage, the certificate's regulatory clarity.

Factor Certificate

Factor Certificate · 2× / 3× / 5×

Daily-reset fixed-leverage exposure. NAV compounds daily — mathematically the securitised perpetual: replicates perp funding mechanics inside a security wrapper.

Sells because: retail-friendly perp economics, no funding-rate volatility surprises.

Turbo Certificate

Turbo Certificate

KO variant with a fixed expiry (1–12 months) and rolling funding cost. Known maturity, known cost, defined risk.

Sells because: retail prefers a finite horizon to a continuously-funded position.

Mini Future

Mini Future

No knock-out barrier, continuous funding, perpetual maturity. The closest direct analog to a crypto perpetual — but issued as a regulated security.

Sells because: traders who want the perp shape without the unregulated venue.

Product excursion · 2 of 3 · Yield with protection

Yield, downside-aware, tokenized.

Treasury allocators, exchange balance sheets, and prosumer wealth segments are all asking the same question: where does conservative on-chain yield live? These are the answers.

Principal Protected Note

Principal Protected Note (PPN)

Capital back at maturity with upside participation in an underlying — the conservative cash alternative for risk-averse capital.

Buyer: treasury, family office, idle stablecoin balances looking for non-zero yield with a hard floor.

BRC · Reverse Convertible

Barrier Reverse Convertible (BRC)

Enhanced coupon now, full repayment at maturity unless the underlying breaches the barrier — in which case you receive the asset. Classic European structured yield product.

Buyer: investors with a view that the underlying trades sideways or modestly up. High coupon is the hook.

Bonus Certificate

Bonus Certificate

Pay a conditional bonus (e.g. +15%) at maturity if the underlying never touches the barrier during the term. Otherwise standard exposure.

Buyer: long-the-underlying investors who want a yield kicker on the path-dependent risk.

Covered Call Note

Covered Call Strategy Note

Yield-enhanced exposure to an underlying with the upside capped at a strike. Packages a systematic options strategy into a single token.

Buyer: holders of the underlying who would otherwise overwrite manually — now passive.

Product excursion · 3 of 3 · Relative value & exotic

Sophisticated payoffs, on-chain.

Beyond the headline leverage and yield products, the same issuance stack supports relative-value and path-dependent payoffs the on-chain world doesn't yet have. Each one is a new SKU on the same rails.

Outperformance Certificate

Outperformance Certificate

Pays the spread of one underlying versus a benchmark — BTC vs ETH, BTC vs Gold, BTC vs SPX. Pure cross-asset relative-value trade in a single instrument.

Buyer: macro views without leg-by-leg execution.

Shark Fin

Shark Fin

Double-barrier KO with a multiplier set so payoff ≈ 1 / P(in range). High leverage on a range-bound view; zero payoff outside.

Buyer: investors with a specific view on realised volatility / range.

Carry Trade Note

Carry Trade Note

Packages crypto funding-rate / basis exposure into a single token. Yield derived from market microstructure, not directional risk.

Buyer: institutional allocators wanting carry without operating the trade.

End of excursion. Every product on the last three slides shares one architecture: Liechtenstein-issued security token, distributed OTC through licensed brokers, settled and serviced on Anduin rails. New SKUs cost weeks of engineering, not quarters — which is why the pipeline keeps widening without the build cost compounding.

Pipeline reality

Slower than we'd hoped — but never going backwards.

What's true

Every cycle is taking longer than our base case.

  • Regulated counterparties run quarter-long internal cycles, not week-long ones.
  • Decisions need legal, risk, and exec sign-off in parallel.
  • Commitments take months to convert into integrated, live flow.
  • Honest framing: against the timeline we showed twelve months ago, we are behind.
What's also true

Movement is one-directional — toward us.

  • Issuance pipeline went from zero signed to three signed this quarter.
  • Stellar Foundation chose us — inbound, foundation-tier.
  • Bitvavo emerged as a hot, fast-moving lending opportunity alongside the committed Revolut conversation.
  • Zero counterparties have dropped. Every named relationship is still in.

The honest read: measured against the original timeline, we're behind. Measured against where regulated-finance B2B sales actually clears — multiple signed anchors, a widening Tier 1, a foundation-tier inbound, and zero churn — we are early on a curve that's now legibly going up and to the right.
